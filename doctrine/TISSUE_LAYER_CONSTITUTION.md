_# TISSUE_LAYER_CONSTITUTION.md

---

## I. Layer Definition

A **Tissue** is a cross-category capability assembly within the WebWaka Biological Architecture. It is a composition of Cells, acting as a coordination layer. It is not a primitive behavior, nor is it a business domain.

Tissue = First level where cross-category composition is permitted.

---

## II. Structural Distinction

-   **Organelle:** primitive behavior
-   **Cell:** reusable single-category capability
-   **Tissue:** multi-cell coordination (may span categories)
-   **Organ:** business capability domain

It is explicitly forbidden for a Tissue to:
-   Contain raw organelles directly.
-   Implement business domain logic.
-   Redefine categories.

---

## III. Structural Invariants

1.  A Tissue MUST be composed of Cells.
2.  A Tissue MAY span multiple categories.
3.  A Tissue MUST NOT contain business-domain semantics.
4.  A Tissue MUST NOT redefine category boundaries.
5.  A Tissue MUST NOT implement UI.
6.  A Tissue MUST NOT bypass Cell abstraction.

---

## IV. Boundary Clarifications

A Tissue does NOT define or implement:
-   No pricing models.
-   No customer semantics.
-   No regulatory interpretation.
-   No branding rules.
-   No system-level deployment logic.

---

## V. Amendment Rules

No structural modification to the Tissue layer is permitted without:
-   A formal proposal.
-   A full structural audit.
-   Founder ratification.

---
