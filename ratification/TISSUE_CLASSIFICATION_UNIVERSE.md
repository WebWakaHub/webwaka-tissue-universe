# TISSUE CLASSIFICATION UNIVERSE

---

## I. Declaration

This document declares that Tissue classification is based on assembly intent, not primitive domain category.

**Tissues are NOT categorized by the 18 organelle domains.**

**Tissues are categorized by structural assembly purpose.**

---

## II. Canonical Classification Axes

The following four canonical Tissue classifications are hereby declared:

### 1. Cross-Functional Tissues

**Definition:**  
Assemblies that coordinate multiple Cells across categories to deliver a unified capability surface.

---

### 2. Lifecycle Tissues

**Definition:**  
Assemblies that coordinate Cells across stages of creation, evolution, and termination.

---

### 3. Structural Boundary Tissues

**Definition:**  
Assemblies that enforce separation, mediation, or translation across structural boundaries.

---

### 4. Scale & Deployment Tissues

**Definition:**  
Assemblies responsible for horizontal scaling, distribution, replication, or deployment orchestration.

---

## III. Structural Rules

1. A Tissue MUST belong to exactly one classification axis.
2. A Tissue MUST NOT redefine category logic.
3. A Tissue MUST NOT implement business-domain semantics.
4. Cross-axis blending is prohibited.
5. Classification renaming requires constitutional amendment.

---

## IV. Amendment Rule

Any modification to this classification universe requires the same formal amendment protocol as other constitutional layers.
